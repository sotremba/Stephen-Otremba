About Me: Rayden Y. Chia, OSCE

I am a highly self-motivated Information Technology (IT) professional whose interests lie in the areas of information security, systems engineering and software security. My current foci include vulnerability research, exploit development, malware analysis as well as penetration testing and how it plays an integral part in the security posture of an entity. Additionally, I am also exploring the area of IT governance, which includes the assessment and evaluation of IT controls and frameworks, as well as the formulation of policies at various levels to facilitate information assurance.

I also take an avid interest in the design, implementation and monitoring of secure networked systems for organizations. I have experience in the configuration and maintenance of secure networked systems that comply with necessary industry standards and requirements. I have also worked on enterprise solutions with security as a key consideration in its design and implementation.

Software Security Research Intern

Singapore University of Technology and Design (SUTD) • Singapore • Aug 2013 - Dec 2013

  • The goal of my research was to develop novel techniques to thwart efforts in software reverse engineering on PE32 and MSIL binaries. During the course of R&D, topics such as the following were evaluated with depth:
  • - Advanced Code Obfuscation
  • - Malware Techniques
  • - Reverse Engineering of PE32 and MSIL binaries
  • - Packers, Cryptors, Protectors and Thin VMs
  • The research culminated in a C/C++ framework that enables instruction-wise on-the-fly encryption/decryption of a chosen code segment in the binary (i.e. only one instruction is valid and executable at any point during execution). Empirical testing showed that there was approximately 30% overhead added to the execution time of the protected code segment. The final solution made use of multiple tricks at various levels of the compiler, debugger and Windows operating system.
